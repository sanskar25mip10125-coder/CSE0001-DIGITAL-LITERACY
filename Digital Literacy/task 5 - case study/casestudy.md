## UPI / Online Payment Fraud – Case Study

UPI fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime in India, especially among students and regular smartphone users. It involves tricking individuals into transferring money or sharing sensitive financial details.

In a typical case, a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or even a known person. First, they gain the victim’s trust by creating urgency, such as claiming that the bank account will be blocked or a refund needs to be processed. Next, they send a payment request or a suspicious link and instruct the victim to approve it. Many people mistakenly believe that entering their UPI PIN is required to receive money, whereas it actually authorizes a payment.

Students and elderly people are often targeted because they may not fully understand how digital payment systems work. Once the victim shares their details or approves the request, the money is instantly transferred to the fraudster’s account.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes misuse of personal data. Recovery of money is difficult if not reported immediately. This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful use of digital payment platforms.
